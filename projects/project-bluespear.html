Project Overview

Project BlueSpear is a long-term Aero Mavs concept focused on developing a high-altitude rocket system with the goal of reaching near-space flight conditions. The project required early-stage planning around mission objectives, launch feasibility, safety requirements, technical organization, and future subsystem development.

Because of the scale and complexity of the concept, the project emphasized systems-level thinking. Rather than treating the rocket as only a hardware build, the work involved considering mission planning, launch operations, safety constraints, documentation needs, and the long-term structure required to support a student-led aerospace program.

My Role

What I Learned

Project BlueSpear helped me understand the importance of systems engineering in ambitious aerospace projects. A high-altitude rocket concept requires more than a strong technical idea. It also requires requirements, safety planning, launch operations research, documentation, team continuity, and realistic project phasing.

This project also strengthened my understanding of how early planning decisions shape later engineering work. Defining goals, constraints, and documentation expectations early makes it easier for a team to transition from concept development into subsystem design, testing, and integration.

Future Improvements

Future work on Project BlueSpear could include developing a formal mission requirements document, creating subsystem-level requirements, expanding flight simulation work, defining a test program, building a risk matrix, and creating a staged technical roadmap for future team members.

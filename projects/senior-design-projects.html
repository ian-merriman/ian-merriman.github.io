Project Overview

My senior design work focused on the reverse engineering and evaluation of the Grumman X-29, an experimental forward-swept wing aircraft. The project required applying aerospace design methods to understand the aircraft configuration, mission assumptions, stability and control behavior, and vehicle-level trade space.

This work combined technical research, low-fidelity analysis, MATLAB-based calculations, aircraft design methods, and written documentation. The project helped me practice working as part of a larger design team while contributing discipline-specific analysis in synthesis and stability and control.

My Role

I contributed to the synthesis and stability and control portions of the project. My work involved supporting vehicle-level configuration evaluation, reviewing technical sources, developing analysis methods, and documenting how aircraft geometry and assumptions influenced stability, trim, and control behavior.

I also helped connect the discipline-level analysis to the broader team design process by organizing findings into report-ready explanations, figures, and technical discussion.

What I Learned

This senior design work strengthened my understanding of how aircraft configuration, geometry, stability, and control are connected. It also helped me see how important assumptions are in early-stage aerospace design, especially when using low-fidelity methods to evaluate a complex vehicle.

I also learned how to communicate technical analysis in a team setting. A useful engineering result needs to be documented clearly, connected to the project objective, and presented with enough context for other disciplines to use it.

Future Improvements

Future improvements could include expanding the stability and control analysis, adding dynamic mode evaluation, improving trim and control authority modeling, validating against additional NASA flight-test data, and creating a GitHub repository with organized scripts, figures, assumptions, and documentation.
